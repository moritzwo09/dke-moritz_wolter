1. INTRODUCTION

27

The impact of heavy metals like copper (Cu) and lead (Pb) pollution of water sources has far

28

reaching environmental and health consequences because of their non-biodegradability, high

29

toxic, and bioaccumulation in aquatic ecosystems.1-2 Expose and excessive intake of these metals

30

can lead to several types of metabolic and physiological disorders in humans, animals, and

31

plants.2-3 The source of these metals can be attributed to different industries, mineral processing,

32

metal plating and battery manufacturing among others4, making the efficient removal strategies

33

of these metal ions a critical global challenge.

34

Several treatment methods have been used to remove heavy metal ions from aqueous media,

35

including evaporation, electrodeposition, chemical precipitation, membrane separation, and ion

36

exchange, however, they are often suffering from high operational costs, incomplete ion removal,

37

ineffective, and harmful to the environment.5-6 Adsorption-based technologies are widely

38

recognized and considered as a popular choice alternative to aforementioned methods for their

39

cost-effectiveness, flexibility in design, operational simplicity, reversibility, and high removal

40

efficiency.7 The efficiency of adsorption method is largely constrained by the ability to design

41

adsorbents with the necessary physical and chemical functionalities tailored for the target

42

contaminant. Porous materials such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs)8-9 have been shown to

43

be very effective adsorbents10-11 for heavy metal ions because of their high surface area, tunable

44

pore size, and numerous number of active sites. MOFs are advanced inorganic-organic hybrid

45

materials12 that allow for the systematic buildup of metal clusters and organic linkers into

46

topologically well-defined porous architectures. Reticular chemistry13-17 has been developed as a

47

discipline over the last decade and topology diagrams and synthetic methodologies are now

48

available to create a vast number of MOFs with potential applications. Recent developments
3

https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-43nmb ORCID: https://orcid.org/0000-0003-2267-5765 Content not peer-reviewed by ChemRxiv. License: CC BY-NC-ND 4.0

49

have led to the synthesis of numerous MOFs with high selectivity and affinity towards the target

50

pollutants, Cu(II) and/or Pb(II), based on variation in surface area, pore size, and chemistry for

51

the environmental remediation.10, 18 MOF-5, a cubic MOF compound synthesized from zinc and

52

1,4-terephthalic acid, showed a maximum uptake of 289.9 mg/g on Cu(II) and 517.6 mg/g on

53

Pb(II) at a pH of 5.2.19 Upon changing the zinc to copper, Rahimi and Mohaghegh found that the

54

copper-terephthalate MOF, Cu(tpa)(dmf), only showed an uptake of Cu(II) and Pb(II) with 71.9

55

mg/g and 35.1 mg/g at a pH of 7, respectively.20 Shooto et al. reported a Cadmium-based MOF,

56

which is non-porous to N2, exhibiting a 183.4 mg/g uptake of Cu(II) and 171.4 mg/g removal of

57

Pb(II) from aqueous solutions without cadmium ion leaching.21 Ghaedi et al. also used a

58

Cadmium-based MOF, MOF-2(Cd), for the removal of Cu(II) and Pb(II) in aqueous solutions.

59

They found that the MOF-2(Cd) was not only chemical and thermal stable but also had a

60

maximum monolayer adsorption capacity of 434.8 and 769.2 mg/g, respectively, towards Cu(II)

61

and Pb(II) at a pH 6 aqueous solution.22 Other MOF materials, such as ZIF-823, UiO-6624-26, Cd-

62

MOF-7427, etc., were also investigated for their potential use in Cu(II) and/or Pb(II) ions

63

removal. Despite extensive research on MOFs for wastewater treatment, many exhibit significant

64

limitations, including low sensitivity for target ions, insufficient adsorption capacity, and

65

vulnerability to humidity and acidity due to the hydrolysis of metal ligand bonds.28 Additionally,

66

their synthesis methods are often not optimized for cost-effective large-scale heavy metal ion

67

removal, making practical application challenging.29 Therefore, developing selective adsorbent

68

with high adsorption capacity, rapid kinetics, and cost-effective is crucial for advancing

69

wastewater treatment technologies.

70

Zinc-based Calgary Framework 20 (CALF-20) is a MOF that has recently demonstrated for

71

selective separations due to the high chemical robustness and precise structural design30. Notable
4

https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-43nmb ORCID: https://orcid.org/0000-0003-2267-5765 Content not peer-reviewed by ChemRxiv. License: CC BY-NC-ND 4.0

72

is that while many other MOFs are unstable in humid conditions, and are thus not suitable for use

73

in steam or wet acid gases environments, CALF-20 is stable and performs well in such

74

conditions, which makes it suitable for real life use.30-31 The structure of this zinc-based MOF

75

reveals a 3D nanoscale lattice with high pore interconnection, a feature that would suggest

76

suitability for adsorption of heavy metal ions. Lin et al.30 documented that a high product yields

77

of up to 90% and an exceptional space-time yield of 550 kg/m3 day can be achieved by using

78

methanol and water as solvents, as well as low cost, commercially available starting materials. In

79

2023, BASF in collaboration with Canadian company Svante had successfully scaled up CALF-

80

20 from lab-scale to industrial scale with an estimation of more than 200 tons per year32 by using

81

a simple low-temperature process in accordance with green chemistry principles. With the

82

hundred-ton scale production, the price of CALF-20 is estimated to be $20-30 per kg32-33.

83

Here, for the first time, we report the heavy metal adsorption performance of CALF-20 in both

84

batch and continuous flow systems, focusing on its applicability for Cu(II) and Pb(II) removal

85

(Scheme 1). Coupled aqueous coordination chemistry and supramolecular assembly, a simple,

86

green, and scalable synthesis approach of CALF-20 at ambient temperature and pressure was

87

achieved. The effective of pH, initial concentration, contact time, and coexisting cations on the

88

adsorption process were evaluated. As-synthesized CALF-20 demonstrated significant Cu(II) and

89

Pb(II) capture capabilities compared to its MOF analogues, due to its microporous structure,

90

stable framework, and high surface area. The adsorption isotherms, kinetics, reusability, and

91

adsorption mechanism were analyzed and investigated. With this comprehensive evaluation of

92

CALF-20's feasibility in Cu(II) and Pb(II) removal, this work aims to demonstrate its potential

93

for wastewater remediation applications.

4. CONCLUSION

424

This study presents the first comprehensive investigation into the use of CALF-20, a zinc-based

425

MOF, for the efficient removal of Cu(II) and Pb(II) from aqueous solutions. Synthesized at room

426

temperature using water and methanol as green solvents and low-cost, commercially available

427

reagents, CALF-20 offers a highly cost-effective and scalable alternative to conventional MOF

428

adsorbents. It demonstrated outstanding adsorption capacities of 250 mg/g for Cu(II) and 833

https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-43nmb ORCID: https://orcid.org/0000-0003-2267-5765 Content not peer-reviewed by ChemRxiv. License: CC BY-NC-ND 4.0

429

mg/g for Pb(II) at pH 5.3, respectively, outperforming many MOF adsorbents previously

430

reported. Kinetic studies confirmed the pseudo-second-order behavior, while adsorption isotherm

431

followed the Langmuir model. In a complex multi-ion environment, CALF-20 maintained good

432

ability to remove multiple metal ions simultaneously, removing over 99% of Cu(II) and 82% of

433

Pb(II), even at low dosages. Notably, the material retained over 90% of its capacity after five

434

adsorption-desorption cycles, highlighting its long-term stability and reusability. The combined

435

advantages of economic synthesis, industrial scalability, excellent adsorption performance, and

436

regeneration capability position CALF-20 as a promising candidate for real-world industrial

437

water treatment and environmental remediation.
